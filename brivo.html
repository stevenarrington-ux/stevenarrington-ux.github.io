The Challenge

Aligning Brivo Teams Around Emerging Tech

When I joined Brivo, the product organization was operating in silos. Different teams had built different UI patterns, there was no shared language between design and engineering, and the product experience reflected years of bolt-on decisions rather than a coherent vision.

The core challenge wasn't just visual — it was organizational. I needed to earn the trust of product leadership, align engineering around a shared component system, and do this while the company was simultaneously accelerating into AI-powered features.

🔴 Before

  • Fragmented UI across 17+ product areas
  • No shared design system or token library
  • Design and engineering without shared language
  • Low user satisfaction on key workflows

🟢 After

  • Unified design system across all product areas
  • Semantic token library bridging Figma and code
  • Design–engineering collaboration model in place
  • 40% lift in overall user satisfaction
How I Led This

Establishing Annual Strategic Planning Process

The first thing I built wasn't a component — it was a planning process. I introduced annual design strategy cycles that aligned the design org with product roadmap priorities, created space for design-led initiatives, and gave leadership visibility into how design investment mapped to business outcomes.

This gave the team a shared sense of direction, gave stakeholders confidence in design's role, and gave me the influence to drive systemic change across 17 product surfaces simultaneously.

Build Community & Build Alignment

Alongside process, I invested in team culture — design crits, cross-functional reviews, and regular 1:1s that surfaced blockers early. Building psychological safety meant designers took more risks, challenged assumptions, and pushed back on scope creep.

  • Established regular design critique sessions across the team
  • Created cross-functional design reviews with PM and engineering leadership
  • Implemented a structured onboarding process for new designers
  • Launched a design principles framework used across all product decisions

Unblocking Ideas Before Writing Them Down

I made it easy for designers to go from idea to testable prototype without waiting for product approval. By establishing rapid prototyping norms and giving designers protected time for exploratory work, the team generated ideas that later became roadmap items — including several AI feature directions that shipped to production.
